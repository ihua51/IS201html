How to Live Aloha

Things to Know About Living in Hawaii





  1. The average temperature in Hawaii is around 80 to 85 degrees Fahrenheit. If you stay long enough to adjust to the warm weather, however, you'll still want some warm clothes if the temperature drops!
  2. If you stay on the West ("Leeward") side of any island, it is going to be hot and dry. The East ("Windward") side of each island, by contrast, is wet and very humid.
  3. In Hawaii, many of the local people speak a variant of English called Hawaiian pidgin. Watch the youtube video below to get an idea of what it sounds like!
  4. Some of the best beaches in Hawaii include:
    • Waikiki Beach
    • Waimea Bay
    • Hanalei Bay
    • Poipu Beach
  5. The living expenses in Hawaii are much higher than the continental United States. See the visualization below to see how much more expensive many common costs are in Hawaii.
  6. Be careful if you whistle at night in Hawaii-- a local legend tells of a ghostly king still roaming the islands who may be summoned with his armies if he hears whistling in the night!
  7. Hawaii is full of natural beauty, but the real beauty is in the people! The love language of many locals is service and giving gifts, so make sure never to come to a lu'au empty handed!
